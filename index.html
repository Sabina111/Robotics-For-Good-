About the Robotics for Good Youth Challenge

The Robotics for Good Youth Challenge 2025 is a UN-based educational robotics competition held every two years, focusing on global challenges where robotics can advance the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). It encourages participants to design, build, and program robots that solve real-world problems while fostering teamwork, problem-solving, and technical skills. The competition promotes inclusivity through open-source hardware and software, allowing unrestricted robot assembly and coding while emphasizing sustainability by encouraging the use of recycled materials. In Nepal, the 2024-2025 theme centers on disaster response, addressing the country's vulnerability to natural disasters while supporting SDGs related to education, innovation, and sustainable communities. Additionally, the AI for Good - Youth Challenge 2025 focuses on AI-driven robotics solutions, with this year's challenge requiring participants to develop a robot that uses computer vision to identify and sort objects by color for efficient and automated waste management. By integrating AI, automation, and robotics, the initiative enhances efficiency, reduces errors, and improves safety in recycling processes, inspiring youth to explore AI applications in sustainability.

About Robotics Association of Nepal (RAN)

The Robotics Association of Nepal (RAN) is Nepal's leading nonprofit organization dedicated to advancing IT, Science, Technology, Robotics, and Artificial Intelligence (AI) among government bodies, businesses, students, and tech enthusiasts. Since its establishment in 2010, RAN has driven robotics, automation, and drone technology, fostering research, innovation, and skill development while tackling national challenges through cutting-edge solutions. RAN has pioneered drone technology, leading initiatives like the Seed Bombing Drone project with UNDP Nepal, mapping landslide-prone areas and deploying seed bombs for reforestation, as well as KrishakCopter, funded by NAST, which trains farmers in drone-based agriculture, currently being expanded for large-scale spraying in Koshi Province.RAN has actively conducted drone training since 2012 with Aerospace India and Tri-Chandra Campus, introducing drone-based inaugurations at major events like NADA Auto Show, CAN Info-Tech, and National ICT Day. It fosters innovation and entrepreneurship through events like Yantra Science, Tech, and Entrepreneurship Festival and the ADB Hackathon for Sanitation Workers, which secured $40,000 in funding for high-tech sanitation startups. RAN also plays a key role in AI and robotics research, hosting AI Conference for Prosperous Nepal 2024 and the International Conference on Robotics and AI 2024, collaborating with MoEST, MoCIT, MoICS, and Nepal's Prime Minister's Office. In urban development, RAN partners with Kathmandu Metropolitan City (KMC), Good City Foundation (GCF), and the World Economic Forum (WEF) for Smart City Master Planning. The AI for Good – Youth Challenge 2025 focuses on AI-driven waste management, challenging participants to develop an AI-powered color-based sorting robot, promoting efficiency, sustainability, and ethical innovation. RAN envisions creating an Intelligent Robotics Industry in Nepal by 2045.
